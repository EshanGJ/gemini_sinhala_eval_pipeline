### **English Translation Summary (For Context)**
*Note: This is a summary of the meaning, not a literal word-for-word translation.*

The text begins by stating that many people are famous for lying. They speak harsh words and babble without care. Even among those who observe *Sil* (precepts), this habit may exist. It notes that many lie even while observing Sil. A person observing Sil should not lie even in casual conversation. It is better to remain in strict silence than to speak untruths. If one speaks, it must be the truth and righteous speech.

The text references a verse from the *Dhammapada* (Verse 176) stating there is no sin a liar cannot commit.
*   **Pali Verse:** *Ekam dhammam atitassa - musavadissa jantuno...*
*   **Meaning:** For the person who transgresses the one law (of truth), who lies, and who has abandoned the next world, there is no evil that cannot be done.

The text concludes the section on lying by advising that one should be determined to speak only soft, sweet, and righteous words that earn the respect of others.

Finally, it introduces the **5th Precept**:
*   **Pali:** *Surameraya majjapama datthana veramani sikkhapadam samadiyami.*
*   **Sinhala Meaning:** I observe the precept of refraining from drinking intoxicating drinks and drugs which lead to heedlessness/negligence.
